# News Impact Analysis Framework

## Event Classification

### 1. Earnings Announcements

#### Types
```
Quarterly Earnings:
- Revenue vs expectations
- EPS vs expectations
- Guidance updates
- Margin trends

Special Items:
- One-time gains/losses
- Asset sales
- Restructuring charges
- Legal settlements
```

#### Impact Assessment
```
Beat/Miss Magnitude:
- >10% surprise: High impact
- 5-10% surprise: Medium impact
- <5% surprise: Low impact

Guidance Changes:
- Raise guidance: Strong positive
- Maintain guidance: Neutral
- Lower guidance: Strong negative

Quality Check:
- Are beats sustainable?
- Are misses temporary?
- Cash flow vs earnings?
```

#### Short-term (1-3 months)
```
Scenario A (Strong Beat + Raise):
- Stock up 5-15%
- Follow-through buying
- Analyst upgrades likely
- Duration: 1-2 weeks

Scenario B (Beat but Lower Guidance):
- Stock flat to down
- "Sell the news"
- Concerns about sustainability
- Duration: Mixed

Scenario C (Miss + Lower Guidance):
- Stock down 10-20%
- Panic selling
- Analyst downgrades
- Duration: 2-4 weeks
```

#### Long-term (3+ months)
```
Key Questions:
- Is the trend changing?
- Are fundamentals shifting?
- Is the market re-rating?
- New equilibrium price?

Example:
- Earnings miss due to temporary issue → Recovery
- Earnings miss due to structural problem → Permanent decline
```

### 2. Policy/Regulatory News

#### Types
```
Government Policy:
- Tax changes
- Industry regulations
- Subsidies/support
- Trade policies

Regulatory Actions:
- Antitrust investigations
- Fines/penalties
- Approval/denials
- Compliance requirements
```

#### Impact Assessment Framework
```
Severity:
- Major: Industry restructuring
- Moderate: Significant cost impact
- Minor: Operational tweaks

Scope:
- Company-specific
- Industry-wide
- Market-wide

Duration:
- Permanent structural change
- Temporary adjustment
- One-time event
```

#### Short-term Analysis
```
Positive Policy:
- Immediate rally (5-20%)
- Sector rotation into beneficiaries
- Volume surge
- May fade if already priced in

Negative Policy:
- Immediate sell-off (5-20%)
- Sector rotation out
- Panic selling
- May recover if overdone
```

#### Long-term Analysis
```
Key Considerations:
- Implementation timeline
- Enforcement strength
- Industry adaptation
- Competitive dynamics

Example:
- New subsidy → Long-term benefit but short-term overreaction
- Regulatory crackdown → Structural headwind but may create buying opportunity
```

### 3. M&A News

#### Types
```
Acquisitions:
- Strategic acquisitions
- Financial acquisitions
- Asset purchases

Mergers:
- Stock-for-stock
- Cash + stock
- All-cash

Divestitures:
- Spin-offs
- Asset sales
- Carve-outs
```

#### Target Company Impact
```
Acquisition Premium:
- Typical: 20-40% premium
- Cash deals: Immediate 20-40% up
- Stock deals: 10-25% up (arbitrage)

Factors Affecting Premium:
- Strategic fit
- Competitive bidding
- Regulatory approval
- Financing certainty
```

#### Acquirer Impact
```
Positive:
- Strategic rationale clear
- Accretive to earnings
- Synergies identified
- Stock-for-stock (no cash out)

Negative:
- Overpaying (too high premium)
- Integration risks
- Debt burden increase
- Dilutive to earnings
```

#### Competitor Impact
```
Positive:
- "Next target" speculation
- Industry consolidation theme
- Defensive M&A rumors

Negative:
- Increased competition
- Margin pressure
- Market share loss
```

#### Timeline
```
Day 1: Announcement (gap up/down)
Week 1-4: Due diligence, analyst reactions
Month 2-6: Regulatory review, financing
Month 6-12: Closing, integration

Arbitrage opportunity: Spread between offer and market price
```

### 4. Product/Technology News

#### Types
```
New Product Launch:
- Major product
- Product line extension
- Service launch

Technology Breakthrough:
- Patent granted
- R&D milestone
- Partnership announcement

Product Issues:
- Recalls

- Discontinuations
```

#### Impact Assessment
```
Market Size:
- TAM (Total Addressable Market)
- Market penetration potential
- Growth runway

Competitive Advantage:
- First-mover advantage
- Patent protection
- Switching costs
- Network effects

Timing:
- Market readiness
- Competitive response
- Adoption curve
```

#### Short-term
```
Positive News:
- Initial excitement (5-15%)
- Hype cycle
- Media coverage
- May fade if no follow-through

Negative News:
- Immediate hit (5-15%)
- Reputation damage
- Customer concerns
- Recovery depends on severity
```

#### Long-term
```
Success Factors:
- Execution capability
- Market acceptance
- Competitive response
- Scalability

Key Metrics to Watch:
- Sales ramp
- Customer feedback
- Market share
- Margin impact
```

### 5. Management Changes

#### Types
```
CEO/CFO Changes:
- Planned retirement
- Forced resignation
- Unexpected departure

Board Changes:
- New directors
- Activist involvement
- Governance issues

Key Personnel:
- C-suite changes
- Division heads
- Technical leaders
```

#### Impact Assessment
```
Reason for Change:
- Positive: Growth, promotion, new direction
- Negative: Performance, scandal, disagreement

Track Record:
- Past performance
- Industry experience
- Strategic vision
- Execution ability

Continuity:
- Company culture
- Strategic direction
- Key relationships
- Team stability
```

#### Short-term
```
Positive Change:
- Stock up 3-10%
- Confidence boost
- Analyst approval

Negative Change:
- Stock down 3-10%
- Uncertainty premium
- Analyst concerns

Uncertain:
- Mixed reactions
- Wait and see
- Volatility increase
```

#### Long-term
```
Key Questions:
- New strategy direction?
- Execution capability?
- Cultural fit?
- Stakeholder reactions?

Timeline:
- 3 months: Initial assessment
- 6 months: Strategy clarity
- 12 months: Results visible
```

### 6. Macro Events

#### Types
```
Economic Data:
- GDP growth
- Inflation (CPI/PPI)
- Employment
- PMI
- Retail sales

Central Bank:
- Rate decisions
- QE/QT
- Forward guidance
- Policy shifts

Geopolitical:
- Trade wars
- Sanctions
- Conflicts
- Elections
```

#### Impact Assessment
```
Direct Impact:
- Company fundamentals
- Cost of capital
- Demand environment
- Supply chain

Indirect Impact:
- Market sentiment
- Risk premium
- Sector rotation
- Currency effects

Duration:
- Short-term: 1-3 months
- Medium-term: 3-12 months
- Long-term: 1+ years
```

#### Sector Sensitivity
```
High Sensitivity:
- Financials (interest rates)
- Commodities (inflation)
- Exporters (trade policy)
- Cyclicals (GDP)

Low Sensitivity:
- Utilities (defensive)
- Consumer staples (essential)
- Healthcare (non-cyclical)
- REITs (income)
```

## Analysis Framework

### Step 1: Event Identification
```
What happened?
- Specific details
- Key facts
- Official statements
- Timing

Who is affected?
- Company
- Industry
- Related companies
- Market
```

### Step 2: Severity Assessment
```
Materiality Scale:
1-3: Minor (operational tweaks)
4-6: Moderate (significant impact)
7-9: Major (structural change)
10: Transformational

Factors:
- Financial impact magnitude
- Strategic importance
- Reversibility
- Scope of affected parties
```

### Step 3: Time Horizon Analysis

#### Short-term (1-3 months)
```
Market Reaction:
- Initial move magnitude
- Volume confirmation
- Follow-through potential
- Overreaction check

Key Questions:
- Is this already priced in?
- What's the consensus view?
- Are there hidden aspects?
- What's the sentiment?
```

#### Long-term (3+ months)
```
Fundamental Impact:
- Revenue effect
- Cost structure change
- Competitive position
- Growth trajectory

Key Questions:
- Is this sustainable?
- Does it change the story?
- New fair value?
- Entry/exit points?
```

### Step 4: Scenario Analysis

#### Best Case
```
Conditions:
- Positive interpretation
- Successful execution
- Favorable outcomes

Probability: 20-30%
Impact: +15-30%
```

#### Base Case
```
Conditions:
- Mixed interpretation
- Moderate impact
- Expected outcomes

Probability: 40-50%
Impact: +5-15% or -5-15%
```

#### Worst Case
```
Conditions:
- Negative interpretation
- Failed execution
- Unfavorable outcomes

Probability: 20-30%
Impact: -15-30%
```

### Step 5: Uncertainty Factors
```
Known Unknowns:
- Regulatory approval
- Market acceptance
- Competitive response
- Execution risk

Unknown Unknowns:
- Black swan events
- Unforeseen consequences
- Systemic risks
```

### Step 6: Key Tracking Indicators
```
Leading Indicators:
- Management commentary
- Analyst reports
- Industry data
- Competitor moves

Lagging Indicators:
- Financial results
- Market share
- Stock performance
- Sentiment shifts
```

## Impact on Related Companies

### Supply Chain
```
Upstream Suppliers:
- Positive: Increased demand
- Negative: Customer concentration risk

Downstream Customers:
- Positive: Better products/pricing
- Negative: Supply disruption
```

### Competitors
```
Direct Competitors:
- Positive: "Rising tide lifts all boats"
- Negative: Market share loss

Indirect Competitors:
- Substitution effects
- Technology disruption
- Market expansion/contraction
```

### Industry Ecosystem
```
Complementary Businesses:
- Positive: Synergy effects
- Negative: Disintermediation

Adjacent Industries:
- Spillover effects
- Technology transfer
- Talent movement
```

## Common Analysis Mistakes

### 1. Recency Bias
```
Problem: Overweighting recent news
Solution: Consider full context
```

### 2. Confirmation Bias
```
Problem: Seeking confirming evidence
Solution: Actively seek disconfirming data
```

### 3. Overreaction
```
Problem: Panic selling on bad news
Solution: Assess actual fundamental impact
```

### 4. Underreaction
```
Problem: Ignoring significant news
Solution: Systematic news review process
```

### 5. Ignoring Time Horizon
```
Problem: Confusing short-term and long-term
Solution: Clearly separate analyses
```

```
- Official company announcements

- Mainstream financial media
- Central bank communications
- Government statistics
```

### Medium Reliability
```
- Industry publications
- Analyst reports
- Reputable blogs
- Expert interviews
- Conference calls
```

### Low Reliability
```
- Social media rumors
- Anonymous sources
- Speculative articles
- Unverified leaks
- Forum posts
```

## Decision Framework

### Immediate Actions
```
1. Verify news source and accuracy
2. Assess materiality and scope
3. Check current position (if invested)
4. Review portfolio exposure
5. Monitor market reaction
```

### Investment Decisions
```
If Already Invested:
- Does this change fundamentals?
- Adjust stop loss?
- Add to position?
- Reduce/exit position?

If Not Invested:
- Does this create opportunity?
- Entry point?
- Position size?
- Risk/reward?
```

### Watch List Items
```
Track these metrics:
- Stock price reaction
- Volume patterns
- Analyst revisions
- Management commentary
- Follow-up news
```

---

**Key Principle**: News analysis is about understanding impact, not predicting the future. Focus on scenario planning and risk management rather than certainty.
