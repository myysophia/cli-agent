# Fundamental Analysis Framework

## Analysis Structure

### 1. Company Overview
**Business Model**:
- Core products/services
- Revenue streams
- Customer segments
- Geographic distribution

**Industry Position**:
- Market share
- Competitive ranking
- Industry lifecycle stage
- Barriers to entry

### 2. Financial Ratio Analysis

#### Profitability (Score: 0-20)
```
ROE (Return on Equity):
- >20%: Excellent
- 15-20%: Good
- 10-15%: Average
- <10%: Poor

ROA (Return on Assets):
- >15%: Excellent
- 10-15%: Good
- 5-10%: Average
- <5%: Poor

Gross Margin:
- >50%: Strong pricing power
- 30-50%: Good
- <30%: Competitive pressure

Net Margin:
- >20%: Excellent
- 10-20%: Good
- 5-10%: Average
- <5%: Poor
```

#### Growth (Score: 0-20)
```
Revenue Growth (3-year CAGR):
- >20%: High growth
- 10-20%: Moderate growth
- 5-10%: Slow growth
- <5%: Stagnant

Earnings Growth (3-year CAGR):
- Should match or exceed revenue growth
- Consistency matters

ROE Trend:
- Rising: Improving efficiency
- Stable: Consistent
- Declining: Concerning
```

#### Financial Health (Score: 0-20)
```
Debt-to-Equity Ratio:
- <0.5: Conservative
- 0.5-1.0: Moderate
- >1.0: High debt

Current Ratio:
- >2.0: Very liquid
- 1.5-2.0: Good
- <1.0: Potential issues

Operating Cash Flow:
- Should be positive and growing
- Should exceed net income (quality of earnings)

Free Cash Flow:
- Positive: Financial flexibility
- Negative: Potential concerns
```

#### Valuation (Score: 0-20)
```
P/E Ratio:
- Compare to historical average
- Compare to industry average
- Consider growth rate (PEG)

P/B Ratio:
- <1.5: Potentially undervalued
- 1.5-3.0: Fair value
- >3.0: Potentially overvalued

PEG Ratio:
- <1.0: Undervalued
- 1.0-1.5: Fair
- >1.5: Overvalued

Dividend Yield:
- >3%: Income attractive
- 1-3%: Moderate
- <1%: Growth focus
```

### 3. Management Quality (Score: 0-10)
```
Leadership Track Record:
- Years with current management
- Past performance
- Strategic decisions

Compensation Alignment:
- Insider ownership
- Stock-based compensation
- Performance metrics

Corporate Governance:
- Board independence
- Audit quality
- Transparency
```

### 4. Competitive Advantages (Score: 0-10)
```
Brand Strength:
- Customer loyalty
- Pricing power
- Market recognition

Technology/IP:
- Patents
- R&D capability
- Innovation track record

Network Effects:
- User base size
- Switching costs
- Ecosystem lock-in

Cost Advantages:
- Scale economies
- Process efficiency
- Supply chain control
```

### 5. Long-term Potential (Score: 0-10)
```
Market Opportunity:
- Total addressable market
- Growth runway
- Market penetration

Innovation Pipeline:
- New products/services
- R&D investment
- Technology roadmap

Regulatory Environment:
- Policy support
- Regulatory risks
- Compliance record

ESG Factors:
- Environmental impact
- Social responsibility
- Governance quality
```

## Scoring System

### Total Score: 0-100

| Category | Weight | Score |
|----------|--------|-------|
| Profitability | 20% | 0-20 |
| Growth | 20% | 0-20 |
| Financial Health | 20% | 0-20 |
| Valuation | 20% | 0-20 |
| Management | 10% | 0-10 |
| Competitive Advantage | 5% | 0-5 |
| Long-term Potential | 5% | 0-5 |

### Rating Scale
- **90-100**: Outstanding investment
- **75-89**: Strong investment
- **60-74**: Moderate investment
- **45-59**: Weak investment
- **<45**: Avoid

## Red Flags

### Financial Warning Signs
- Declining margins
- Rising debt
- Negative cash flow
- Accounting irregularities
- Frequent guidance cuts

### Management Warning Signs
- High executive turnover
- Excessive compensation
- Related-party transactions
- Poor capital allocation
- Lack of transparency

### Market Warning Signs
- Deteriorating industry
- New disruptive competitors
- Regulatory headwinds
- Technology obsolescence
- Customer concentration risk

## Analysis Checklist

### Before Investment
- [ ] Understand the business model
- [ ] Analyze 5 years of financials
- [ ] Evaluate management quality
- [ ] Assess competitive position
- [ ] Estimate intrinsic value
- [ ] Consider margin of safety
- [ ] Review risk factors

### Ongoing Monitoring
- [ ] Quarterly earnings review
- [ ] Annual report deep dive
- [ ] Management change tracking
- [ ] Competitive landscape updates
- [ ] Key metric tracking
- [ ] News and event monitoring

## Key Questions to Answer

1. **What does the company do?** (Business understanding)
2. **How does it make money?** (Revenue model)
3. **What are its competitive advantages?** (Moat analysis)
4. **Is management competent and honest?** (Quality assessment)
5. **What is the company worth?** (Valuation)
6. **What are the major risks?** (Risk assessment)
7. **Why is it available at this price?** (Market efficiency check)

## Common Valuation Methods

### Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)
```
Intrinsic Value = Σ [CFt / (1+r)^t]

Where:
CFt = Cash flow in year t
r = Discount rate (WACC)
t = Time period
```

### Comparable Company Analysis
```
P/E Multiple = Industry Average × (1 + Premium/Discount)

Consider:
- Growth differential
- Risk differential
- Size differential
```

### Asset-Based Valuation
```
Net Asset Value = Total Assets - Total Liabilities

Useful for:
- Financial companies
- Real estate companies
- Asset-heavy businesses
```
